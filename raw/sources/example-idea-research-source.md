# Example Idea Research Source

Useful research systems do more than store facts. They also preserve half-formed directions, unanswered questions, and promising idea threads in a way that can drive the next round of reading.

A durable pattern is to keep raw inputs immutable, maintain derived notes separately, and connect them to long-lived problem spaces. Those problem spaces can hold both current understanding and `idea_seeds`, so research is not only archived but also steered.

Two common failure modes are easy to spot. First, teams over-ingest low-signal material and bury insight in clutter. Second, they mix evidence-backed learning with task checklists, which makes the repository less trustworthy as a thinking tool.
